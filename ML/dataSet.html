Feature Breakdown

To build an accurate stress prediction model, we need to understand the variables at play. Here are the 11 key features[cite: 10] we analyzed and how each one actively influences our sleep and stress levels.

Daily Screen Time (hrs)[cite: 10]

Prolonged exposure to the blue light from our screens suppresses melatonin production, throwing off our circadian rhythm and increasing baseline stress.

Phone Usage Before Sleep[cite: 10]

Measured in minutes, scrolling right before bed keeps the brain highly stimulated, delaying the onset of restorative sleep.

Sleep Duration (hrs)[cite: 10]

The core foundation of rest. Consistently sleeping under 5 hours drastically spikes cortisol levels and prevents physical recovery.

Caffeine Intake (cups)[cite: 10]

A central nervous system stimulant. Consuming too much caffeine directly reduces the amount of deep, slow-wave sleep we get at night.

Physical Activity (min)[cite: 10]

Daily exercise acts as a powerful stress buffer. It burns off nervous energy and promotes a faster transition into deep sleep.

Notifications Per Day[cite: 10]

Constant buzzing creates a state of hyper-arousal. High notifications lead to micro-awakenings at night and constant anxiety during the day.

Sleep Quality Score[cite: 10]

A subjective, self-reported measure of how restful and uninterrupted a person felt their sleep was on a given night.

Stress Level (Target)[cite: 10]

The final outcome (scaled 1-10) that our machine learning models are designed to predict based on the combination of all other habits.

Demographics[cite: 10]

Includes Age, Gender, and Occupation. These provide vital context, as stress baselines and lifestyle habits naturally vary across different life stages and job roles.
