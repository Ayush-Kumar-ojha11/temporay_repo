The Problem Statement

Given a person's mobile usage and lifestyle data, our objective is to predict their stress level score (on a scale of 1–10) using advanced machine learning models, progressing from baseline Linear Regression to powerful ensemble methods like XGBoost, rigorously validated using Stratified K-Fold techniques.

The Context Rising Stress Levels

Excessive mobile screen time is increasingly linked to rising stress levels, disrupted sleep patterns, and reduced mental well-being across all age groups.

The Approach Predictive Modeling

This project applies regression-based algorithms to predict stress scores directly from daily habits. By implementing Stratified K-Fold cross-validation, we ensure models like XGBoost and Decision Trees are highly robust and prevent overfitting.

Inside The Dataset

To build an accurate stress prediction model, we analyzed 11 core features to understand how everyday behavioral data actively shapes our physiological rest patterns.

Daily Screen Time

Prolonged exposure to the blue light from screens suppresses natural melatonin production, throwing off our circadian rhythm and increasing baseline stress inputs.

Phone Usage Before Sleep

Late-night smartphone scrolling keeps the brain highly stimulated right before bed, delaying the onset of restorative deep-sleep cycles.

Sleep Duration

The core foundation of physical rest. Consistently sleeping under 5 hours drastically spikes cortisol levels and restricts mental decompression.

Caffeine Intake

A central nervous system stimulant. Consuming too many cups of caffeine directly fragments sleep architecture and shrinks slow-wave cycles.

Physical Activity

Daily active minutes act as a powerful stress buffer, expending excess adrenaline and helping the body transition smoothly into deep sleep.

Notifications Per Day

Constant phone buzzing alerts trigger micro-arousals and an ongoing state of digital hyper-vigilance, degrading daytime productivity.

What Fascinated Me

As I dove deeper into machine learning, there were certain concepts that just completely blew my mind and made me fall in love with the subject.

Mind-Bending Math The Scale of Dimensionality

As humans, we literally can't think or visualize anything beyond 3 dimensions. But these AI models? They work on millions and billions of parameters—essentially millions and billions of dimensions! When I first read about this, it was so incredibly interesting that I went down a rabbit hole and read about it on the internet straight for 30 minutes.

Algorithmic Elegance Backpropagation Efficiency

The sheer efficiency of Backpropagation is amazing. It computes gradients for all weights simultaneously using the chain rule, instead of painstakingly calculating each derivative separately from scratch. It heavily reduces redundant computations and is the exact reason why training massive neural networks is actually feasible today.

The Balancing Act Overfitting vs. Underfitting

It was fascinating to learn about the delicate balance of model training. It's crazy to see how a model can perform flawlessly on the data it knows (overfitting) but completely fail in the real world, or how it can just fail to learn entirely (underfitting). Getting that perfect balance is like an art form.

Data Power The Impact of Preprocessing

The last thing that truly fascinated me was data preprocessing. It's incredible to see how vastly something seemingly simple—like scaling features, handling missing values, or encoding variables—can completely change the final output and the prediction accuracy of your entire model.
