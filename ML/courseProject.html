Machine Learning Project

Sleep Sense

Because your sleep has a story.

The Problem Statement

Given a person's mobile usage and lifestyle data, our objective is to predict their stress level score (on a scale of 1–10) using advanced machine learning models, progressing from baseline Linear Regression to powerful ensemble methods like XGBoost, rigorously validated using Stratified K-Fold techniques.

The Context Rising Stress Levels

Excessive mobile screen time is increasingly linked to rising stress levels, disrupted sleep patterns, and reduced mental well-being across all age groups.

The Approach Predictive Modeling

This project applies regression-based algorithms to predict stress scores directly from daily habits. By implementing Stratified K-Fold cross-validation, we ensure models like XGBoost and Decision Trees are highly robust and prevent overfitting.

Why This Matters

Understanding the non-linear patterns between our digital habits and our physical well-being is crucial for developing healthier lifestyles.

01 Surging Screen Time

Mobile screen time has surged dramatically — average usage now frequently exceeds 6–7 hours daily.

02 Physiological Impact

High screen exposure actively disrupts natural sleep cycles and elevates cortisol, the body's primary stress hormone.

03 AI-Driven Wellness

Machine Learning models can quantify stress risk directly from daily habits — eliminating the immediate need for clinical tests.
