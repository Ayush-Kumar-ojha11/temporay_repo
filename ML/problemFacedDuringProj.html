It wasn't always smooth sailing. Here’s how we actually fixed the problems we faced.

Lessons from the Struggle

Building this project was a real back-and-forth process for the team. We hit some serious walls with data quality and accuracy, and it took a lot of trial and error to actually push past them.

1

The Preprocessing Struggle

The Obstacle: At first, the dataset features were just not balanced at all. Because of that, our early models were barely performing—they were basically just locking onto one or two random functions to make predictions, which gave us really poor accuracy.

The Solution: We had to go back to the drawing board and do multiple iterations on our preprocessing. We focused on functional importance until the model was actually learning from all the features, not just taking shortcuts.

2

Chasing That Accuracy

The Obstacle: Even after fixing the basics, our accuracy improvements were still frustratingly small. We tried throwing everything at it—stacking multiple models, running Stratified K-Fold (for those 5 solid folds), and testing XGBoost—but we still weren't satisfied with the results.

The Solution: It turned out the answer wasn't just in the models themselves, but back in the preprocessing. We started focusing hard on feature scaling and other transformation methods. That was the real game-changer; fixing the quality of the input data did way more for our accuracy than just swapping out models ever did.
