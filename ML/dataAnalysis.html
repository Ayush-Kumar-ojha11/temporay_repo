Finding The Hidden Stories

Data Analysis

Visualizing the silent connections between our lifestyle habits and our stress levels.

The Correlation Heatmap

When I first plotted this correlation matrix after my data preprocessing pipeline, the numbers didn't just show random data—they told a very clear story about modern lifestyle habits. This heatmap became the absolute foundation for selecting the right features for my models.

Key Discoveries

Looking closely at the matrix, three major insights completely dictated the direction of my machine learning approach.

Insight 01 The Inverse Sleep-Stress Link

The strongest negative correlation I found was between Sleep Duration and the target Stress Level. It mathematically proved what we feel: as your sleep hours decrease, your baseline stress score aggressively shoots up. It was the most predictive raw feature in the entire dataset.

Insight 02 The Screen Time Multiplier

Both Daily Screen Time and Phone Usage Before Sleep showed highly positive correlations with mental fatigue and stress. This was exactly why I engineered the Screen-to-Sleep Ratio feature during preprocessing—the combined non-linear effect of these variables was huge.

Insight 03 Caffeine's Hidden Toll

Caffeine Intake had a surprisingly strong positive correlation not just with stress, but directly against sleep quality. This insight heavily influenced my choice to use Decision Trees later on, as Trees are excellent at finding specific threshold values (e.g., "more than 3 cups = high stress risk").
