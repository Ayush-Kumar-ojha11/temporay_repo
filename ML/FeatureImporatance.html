Cutting Through The Noise

Feature Selection

Finding out which habits actually dictate our stress levels.

The XGBoost Reality Check

After looking at the correlation heatmap during my analysis, I needed a more robust way to know which features my XGBoost model actually relied on to make its predictions. Instead of just looking at basic correlation, I used Permutation Importance. By randomly shuffling each feature and seeing how much the model's accuracy dropped, I could pinpoint exactly what matters most in the real world.

My Biggest Takeaways

When the code finally finished running and this chart popped up, I had a few major "Aha!" moments that completely validated my preprocessing efforts.

Takeaway 01 Sleep is the Ultimate Baseline

Sleep Duration didn't just casually top the chart; it absolutely dominated it. It proved that no matter what else you do during the day, your raw hours of sleep are the single most critical factor in predicting your baseline stress levels. Everything else builds on top of this.

Takeaway 02 Engineering Paid Off

This was a massive win for me. I custom-engineered the Screen_to_Sleep_Ratio feature during preprocessing, and seeing it sit at number two on the importance chart felt amazing! It proved that combining variables to capture the relationship between habits is often more powerful than the raw data itself.

Takeaway 03 Demographics Are Noise

I originally assumed that things like Age, Gender, or Occupation would play a huge role in stress. The model proved me completely wrong. Those features sat at the absolute bottom near zero. It taught me a valuable lesson: for this specific problem, your daily lifestyle choices matter infinitely more than who you are or what your job title is.
